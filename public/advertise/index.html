How we work

Editorial principles

✦

No disguised advertising

All sponsored content is clearly labeled. Readers know when content is paid for. We believe transparency builds trust — and trust is the only thing that makes sponsorships worth anything.

✦

Editorial independence

Kayla's opinions are not for sale. Sponsors can place content in this publication — they cannot purchase endorsements or direct editorial positions. The line is non-negotiable.

✦

Category fit required

We only work with brands that make sense in this context — fintech, crypto, technology, books, policy organizations, professional services, and similar. We decline categories that conflict with the editorial voice.

✦

No political campaigns

This site does not accept advertising from political campaigns, PACs, or partisan political organizations. The editorial content speaks for itself.

Sponsorship formats

Each format is built for a different objective. All include a clear "Sponsored by" label.

Newsletter

Newsletter Sponsorship

A dedicated section in Kayla's weekly newsletter — sent to subscribers who opted in specifically for this content. Includes 100 words of sponsor copy, a headline, and a link. Written in Kayla's voice on request.

Sponsored Post

A full editorial post on kaylavbennett.com, clearly labeled as sponsored content. Written to match the editorial quality of the blog. Indexed by search engines, permanent placement, shareable. Best for brands that benefit from long-form explanation.
